# Type E Conceptual or Institutional Skeleton

Status: base template for v2 Phase 4 skeleton-writer. Fork this file into `04_skeleton/section_skeleton.md` and fill it from project evidence. Do not use it as prose.

Rules

- Every paragraph needs one claim, one evidence source, one exhibit link if relevant, and one expected literature category.
- Figure 1 hook belongs in the introduction, usually the last third before the contribution paragraph.
- Claims must match `qa/claim_verification_matrix.md`.
- Unsupported claims return to Phase 3 or Phase 4, not to prose polishing.


## Exhibit spine

- Figure 1 hook: `<fig01_hook_candidate>` in the introduction, normally after motivation and before contribution.
- Table 1 summary: sample or object definition.
- Table 2 main: central result or mechanism.
- Table 3 robustness: claim stability and boundary.

## Section map

### Introduction

Paragraph 1

Claim: Name the institutional gap, market-design friction, or conceptual puzzle.
Exhibit: None or Figure 1 institutional map hook.
Expected literature category: Direct institutional or conceptual threats.

Paragraph 2

Claim: Show the sequence of actors, frictions, rights, or cash flows that creates the problem.
Exhibit: Figure 1 hook candidate, usually schematic or process map.
Expected literature category: Market design, law-finance, or institutional papers.

Paragraph 3

Claim: State the conceptual contribution and what evidence supports it.
Exhibit: Exhibit map preview.
Expected literature category: Closest conceptual comparators.

Paragraph 4

Claim: Define what the paper does not prove empirically.
Exhibit: None.
Expected literature category: Boundary and legal-institutional literature.

### Institutional Setting

Paragraph 1

Claim: Define actors, contracts, rights, obligations, and information flows.
Exhibit: Figure 1 or taxonomy table.
Expected literature category: Institutional setting sources.

Paragraph 2

Claim: Explain why existing categories fail to describe the object.
Exhibit: Taxonomy exhibit.
Expected literature category: Legal, market-design, or institutional taxonomy papers.

Paragraph 3

Claim: State evidence sources and their limits.
Exhibit: Table 1 summary or source map.
Expected literature category: Source and institutional documentation.

### Conceptual Framework

Paragraph 1

Claim: Define the mechanism or design tradeoff.
Exhibit: Mechanism schematic.
Expected literature category: Theory and market design papers.

Paragraph 2

Claim: Map assumptions to observable institutional features.
Exhibit: Framework table.
Expected literature category: Institutional mechanism papers.

Paragraph 3

Claim: Separate conceptual claims from empirical or policy claims.
Exhibit: Claim map.
Expected literature category: Direct critique literature.

### Evidence and Application

Paragraph 1

Claim: Apply the framework to cases, data snippets, or institutional examples.
Exhibit: Table 2 main or case matrix.
Expected literature category: Case or institutional evidence papers.

Paragraph 2

Claim: Show where the framework changes interpretation relative to existing categories.
Exhibit: Figure or table tied to comparison.
Expected literature category: Closest conceptual threats.

Paragraph 3

Claim: State evidence strength and uncertainty for each application.
Exhibit: Evidence matrix.
Expected literature category: Methodological caution sources.

### Robustness of Interpretation

Paragraph 1

Claim: Test the framework against alternative classifications or counterexamples.
Exhibit: Table 3 robustness or taxonomy stress test.
Expected literature category: Alternative frameworks.

Paragraph 2

Claim: Address legal, governance, or implementation objections.
Exhibit: Objection-response table.
Expected literature category: Law, governance, and institutional critique papers.

Paragraph 3

Claim: State where the conceptual framework should not be used.
Exhibit: None or appendix.
Expected literature category: Boundary literature.

### Conclusion

Paragraph 1

Claim: Restate the institutional contribution at verified evidence strength.
Exhibit: None.
Expected literature category: Conceptual and institutional literature.

Paragraph 2

Claim: State empirical tests or policy designs that follow from the framework.
Exhibit: None.
Expected literature category: Future empirical or policy literature.

## Skeleton-writer handoff checks

- [ ] Each paragraph has one claim only.
- [ ] Each claim maps to a verified source, result, or exhibit.
- [ ] Figure 1 hook is selected or three candidates are preserved for Human Gate 2.
- [ ] Literature categories include direct threats, not only background.
- [ ] No paragraph asks writing system to solve a research uncertainty.

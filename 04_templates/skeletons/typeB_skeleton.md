# Type B Real Estate Empirics Skeleton

Status: base template for v2 Phase 4 skeleton-writer. Fork this file into `04_skeleton/section_skeleton.md` and fill it from project evidence. Do not use it as prose.

Rules

- Every paragraph needs one claim, one evidence source, one exhibit link if relevant, and one expected literature category.
- Figure 1 hook belongs in the introduction, usually the last third before the contribution paragraph.
- Claims must match `qa/claim_verification_matrix.md`.
- Unsupported claims return to Phase 3 or Phase 4, not to prose polishing.


## Exhibit spine

- Figure 1 hook: `<fig01_hook_candidate>` in the introduction, normally after motivation and before contribution.
- Table 1 summary: sample or object definition.
- Table 2 main: central result or mechanism.
- Table 3 robustness: claim stability and boundary.

## Section map

### Introduction

Paragraph 1

Claim: Name the real-estate market friction or local-market question.
Exhibit: None or map-style Figure 1 hook.
Expected literature category: Direct real-estate empirics threats.

Paragraph 2

Claim: Show why the setting reveals a margin that standard data miss.
Exhibit: Figure 1 hook candidate, often map, event-time, or market structure visual.
Expected literature category: Urban, housing, or commercial real-estate mechanism papers.

Paragraph 3

Claim: State the empirical design and claim boundary in one direct paragraph.
Exhibit: Exhibit map preview.
Expected literature category: Closest data-setting comparators.

Paragraph 4

Claim: List contributions in data, identification, and market interpretation.
Exhibit: None.
Expected literature category: Recent working papers in real estate and urban economics.

### Setting and Data

Paragraph 1

Claim: Describe geography, unit, time period, and institutional setting.
Exhibit: Figure 1 or Table 1 summary.
Expected literature category: Institutional setting papers.

Paragraph 2

Claim: Define rent, price, footfall, exposure, amenity, or treatment variables.
Exhibit: Table 1 summary.
Expected literature category: Measurement papers and data documentation.

Paragraph 3

Claim: Report sample construction, missingness, and merge support.
Exhibit: Sample flow or appendix table.
Expected literature category: Data-quality and setting comparators.

### Empirical Design

Paragraph 1

Claim: Define main specification, fixed effects, controls, and variation source.
Exhibit: Spec summary or design schematic.
Expected literature category: Identification literature.

Paragraph 2

Claim: Explain identifying assumption and threats in plain terms.
Exhibit: None or balance/pretrend exhibit.
Expected literature category: Direct identification threat papers.

Paragraph 3

Claim: State how spatial or time dependence is handled.
Exhibit: Diagnostics or robustness exhibit.
Expected literature category: Spatial and panel inference papers.

### Main Results

Paragraph 1

Claim: Present the main effect with economic magnitude.
Exhibit: Table 2 main.
Expected literature category: Closest empirical comparators.

Paragraph 2

Claim: Translate the result into the real-estate margin without overclaiming welfare.
Exhibit: Figure or table tied to magnitude.
Expected literature category: Market mechanism literature.

Paragraph 3

Claim: Explain heterogeneity only where pre-specified or clearly diagnostic.
Exhibit: Heterogeneity exhibit if selected.
Expected literature category: Heterogeneity and local-market papers.

### Robustness and Alternative Explanations

Paragraph 1

Claim: Show robustness to fixed effects, controls, sample restrictions, or spatial choices.
Exhibit: Table 3 robustness.
Expected literature category: Empirical robustness standards.

Paragraph 2

Claim: Address alternative explanations such as selection, amenities, composition, or reverse causality.
Exhibit: Mechanism or falsification exhibit.
Expected literature category: Direct threat papers.

Paragraph 3

Claim: State remaining limitations as scope boundaries.
Exhibit: None or appendix.
Expected literature category: Limitation-relevant literature.

### Conclusion

Paragraph 1

Claim: Restate the market-level contribution at verified claim strength.
Exhibit: None.
Expected literature category: Real-estate empirics literature.

Paragraph 2

Claim: State institutional or policy implications only if supported by the design.
Exhibit: None.
Expected literature category: Policy or market-design literature.
